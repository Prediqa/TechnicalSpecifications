01 — Platform Architecture

HIP doesn't replace your stack. It reads it — through industry-standard protocols.

COFLER sits above every source system as a read-and-correlate layer. Each source has a dedicated connector microservice; everything lands in Kafka first; a normalization engine maps it to one internal data model. No rip-and-replace — and every stage is auditable.

01

Connector layer — one microservice per source System

Dedicated adapters: Epic FHIR R4 (App Orchard), Cerner SMART on FHIR, Athenahealth, ABDM for EHR; an X12 EDI parser for 837P/I claims and 835 remittances; X12 270/271 and payer REST for eligibility and prior-auth; SFTP/CSV for HRIS and CMS quality feeds. The connector only delivers — no transformation.

02

Apache Kafka staging queue System

One topic per source, 30-day retention for full replay. Independent consumer groups — normalization engine, ML pipeline, audit-log writer — read in parallel. Malformed payloads route to a dead-letter queue rather than corrupting downstream state.

03

Normalization engine — COFLER Internal Data Model System

Probabilistic patient de-duplication (name + DOB + MRN). Terminology mapping across ICD-10-CM, CPT-4, SNOMED CT, LOINC, RxNorm. Completeness validation with severity scoring and provenance tagging on every record.

G1

Data quality gate — IT / data-engineering lead Human Gate 1

Confirms connector health, reviews the dead-letter queue for persistent failures, approves new-source onboarding, validates terminology-mapping accuracy. Dirty data never enters the engine unchecked.

04

Six lever emitters → signal topic System

Clinical, Operational, Financial, Empathy, Legal, Resource emitters run continuously in parallel, each applying domain detection rules and emitting typed JSON signals with a raw severity weight (1–100) and a confidence score. Signals below 0.65 confidence are excluded from correlation.

Explainability

Every prediction is traceable

Each compound score decomposes into its contributing signals, their base severities, and the named amplifier pair applied. Compliance officers see the rule trace, not a probability with no provenance.

Human-in-the-loop

Low-confidence never auto-acts

Signals under 0.65 confidence are analytics-only. Tier-2 actions require one-click human confirmation with a 10-minute TTL; Tier-1 auto-actions are bounded to non-consequential outputs (drafts, alerts, queue ordering).

Governance

Dual sign-off on every retrain

Model versions live in MLflow. No promotion to production without written sign-off from both the CMIO (clinical) and the data-science lead. The CMIO holds veto. Recalibration is quarterly; amplifier weights monthly.

04 — Correlation Engine Deep-Dive

Three reinforcing moats — none of them mystical.

The "25 years of expertise" is not hand-waving. It is encoded as an amplifier-weights tensor initialized from domain priors, then continuously tuned by human-rejection feedback. Here is exactly how the moat compounds.

Moat 01 — Knowledge graph

A multi-dimensional weight tensor

Clinical × Financial × Operational × Legal × Empathy × Resource. Initialized from 25 years of operational priors, encoding causal relationships between clinical events, billing codes, compliance triggers, and outcomes. Not publicly documented — proprietary, encrypted, service-account-only.

Moat 02 — Feedback loop

Every hospital improves every hospital

Human approvals and rejections at Gates 5 and 6 are logged as training data. After 10+ similar query patterns the denial-pattern library updates; quarterly the XGBoost model retrains on cross-hospital de-identified data. Human rejections are the most valuable signal — they teach the model the institution's risk tolerance.

Moat 03 — Regulatory time

12–18 months of certification lead

SOC 2 Type II, HIPAA BAA, GDPR/DPDPA, and EHR production credentialing take 12–18 months to obtain. A competitor starting today is that far behind on compliance alone — before touching the correlation IP.

Worked example — how a compound score forms
Clinical: LOS anomaly, Unit 4 22 pts + Financial: denial spike, same attending 28 pts = 50 base × C×F amplifier 1.74

compound_score = Σ(severity_weights) × correlation_amplifier = 50 × 1.74 = 87 → CRITICAL tier (80–100). Routed to the CEO and CFO views within 24h; a legal-risk register entry is created; Tier-1 actions execute, Tier-2 actions await confirmation. Matching occurs only between signals sharing an entity identifier within the 45-day rolling window.

04b — Knowledge-Graph Construction

The last opaque element, made concrete.

"25 years of expertise" must resolve into a reproducible process. The amplifier-weights tensor is not hand-tuned from intuition — it is initialized from documented sources, then corrected by outcome data. Here is how nodes, edges, and weights are actually derived.

01

Node derivation — hybrid: codified standards + expert curation

Nodes are clinical events, billing codes, compliance triggers, and operational states. Seeded automatically from standard terminologies — ICD-10-CM, CPT-4, SNOMED CT, CMS NCCI edits, OIG Work Plan — then curated by domain experts to add the relationships those standards don't encode.

02

Edge derivation — causal links from retrospective data

Edges represent causal/correlational relationships (e.g. documentation gap → denial). Candidate edges are proposed from retrospective hospital datasets where co-occurrence is statistically significant, then expert-validated before entering the graph. Each edge carries a provenance tag: data-derived, expert-asserted, or both.

03

Weight initialization — expert prior, then online correction

Amplifier weights (e.g. C×F = 1.74) start as expert priors encoding which cross-lever combinations are most dangerous. They are not static: every false-positive marked at Gate 4 and every human rejection at Gates 5–6 feeds the monthly recalibration, moving weights toward what the data shows.

04

Versioning & governance — fully reproducible

Graph versions and weight tensors are stored in MLflow with dual CMIO + data-science sign-off. Any production weight set can be traced to the dataset and expert decisions that produced it. The graph is reproducible internally — and that auditability is itself a diligence asset.

08 — The Competitive Moat, Honestly

Why couldn't Epic build this in 18 months? The real answer.

"Kafka + XGBoost + a knowledge graph" is not itself defensible — a well-funded team can assemble that stack. The honest moat is not the stack. It is three things a competitor cannot buy with engineering alone.

What a competitor CAN replicate in 12–18 months
  • The technical stack — Kafka, XGBoost, FHIR connectors, a graph database
  • The six-lever taxonomy — once published, the concept is copyable
  • A dashboard layer — UI is not defensible
  • The architecture diagram — this very document describes it
What they CANNOT — the actual moat
  • Calibrated weights from real outcomes. The amplifier tensor is tuned by months of human-rejection feedback across live hospitals. A new entrant starts at expert-prior accuracy and needs the same calibration time — per institution.
  • The cross-hospital feedback flywheel. Each hospital's rejections improve the model for all. This compounds with deployment count — a structural network effect a day-one competitor lacks.
  • Certification + EHR credentialing lead. 12–18 months of SOC 2 / ISO / Epic production access that cannot be parallelized away.
The defensible claim, stated precisely: the moat is not "we have a knowledge graph." It is "our weights are calibrated to real institutional outcomes, that calibration compounds across every deployment, and the regulatory clock has already started for us." The stack is table stakes; the calibration data and the flywheel are the asset.
